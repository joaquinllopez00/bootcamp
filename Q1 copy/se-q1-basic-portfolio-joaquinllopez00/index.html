Background

For all of my primary education, I attended Warren Township schools throughout the East side of Indianapolis. Early on in my school career, I found that my parents looked at this as a disadvantage, given the high crime rate on the East side of Indianapolis. While initially I agreed (as most kids do) with my parents, I started looking at it as an advantage over kids in surrounding areas. I found, especially in high school, that the side of Indianapolis I lived on could be described as what some would call, a melting-pot. Getting to see different cultures everyday, instead of just being apart of a school that is identified by one culture, or one-language even, made me an exteremly open-minded person. Instead of being defensive about what I personally believed, I learned to respect other peoples personal beliefs, and approach every situation with an open mind. If theres one thing I would accredit most of my morals too, it would be the East side of Indianapolis.
